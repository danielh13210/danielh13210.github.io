About me

Hi, I am Daniel. I was born on August 2012. I have a strong interest in electronics and computers throughout my life.

When I was four, my parents gave me a toy to learn electronics, called Snap Circuits. Since then, I began to love electronics a lot. Soon, I had an electronics teacher who taught me about Boolean algebra and logic gates. He introduced me to Arduino which was the first time I learned to program. When I was six, I started learning Python from a book called Hello World.

A year later, I started learning Nand2Tetris. There were two teachers who helped me to finish this course. It took me about a year to finish it. This course helped me to understand how computers work from just one component called "NAND" up to an operating system. I also played NandGame which is based on Nand2Tetris.

I started programming every day since I could use Arduino. I have done many projects, including this portfolio. Since I am homeschooled, I study by myself through books and online learning platforms. Also, I did some hardware projects. The last project was my 8-bit computer, which I built by following a series of YouTube videos. Until now, I keep learning, but sometimes unable to study only by myself because some of the subjects are too hard to study without a mentor, or require facilities that are currently out of my reach. Therefore, I hope that I can be accepted at a university, where I would have a mentor and laboratory facilities.

With my current skillset, I am able to help my grandparents when they have problems with their smartphone or laptop software. I also can help my father with his computer software and connections.

Because I want to learn more about chip & motherboard design, I found your university has a module about chip design in your Computer Engineering programme, whereas, not many universities offer this module. I hope after finishing this programme, I will be able to repair mobile devices, laptops, and desktop computers. By having this knowledge, I will be more useful for people. I also want to make a smartphone, tablet, and laptop that will be more repairable, hence more sustainable.
